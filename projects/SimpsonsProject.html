The Simpsons Series Study¶

The Simpsons is a TV show that has over thirty different seasons, dating back to 1989, when it first started. This resulted in the Simpsons taking away the title of longest running American prime-time scripted television series from Gunsmoke of 20 season. Matt Groening and James L. Brooks have both served as two of the main executive producers of the show. There is currently 32 complete seasons of the Simpsons and confirmed to run untill the end of Season 34. With over 650 episodes there has to be a certain algorythm that makes s great Simpsons episode. For the study done in this file, I will be focusing on all seasons up to season 28, as that is the newest season avaible in the data set. The source of the data set can be found here. These are some of the ways that I am planing to study the date. Is there a correlation between the length of an episode title and the number of views? What year produces the episodes with the largest viewer count? IMDB rating vs. viewer count What seasons had the most views. Were the earlier episodes more popular episodes in a given season?

What is an IMBD Rating or IMDB Vote?

IMDb registered users can cast a vote (from 1 to 10) on every released title in a TV series. Individual votes are then aggregated and summarized as a single IMDb rating, visible on the title's main page. For the following study lets first see if there is somewhat of a relation between the IMBD rating is linked to the number of IMBD votes or the other way around.
